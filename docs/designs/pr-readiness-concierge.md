## Vision

### 10x Check
The 10x version of this business is not a prettier migration-info site. It is the trusted prep layer between confused migrants and expensive licensed help: users know if they are in scope, understand what facts matter in their case, assemble the right evidence, and arrive at licensed review already organized. That creates a wedge where cost drops, confidence rises, and the service can later harden into product infrastructure.

This phase only: launch a narrow concierge prep offer for temporary graduate visa holders from Nepal already in Australia. The goal is not to build the full product now. The goal is to make the manual service trustworthy, repeatable, and measurable.

## Execution Detail

### 1. Pre-Payment Triage Gate
- Trigger: before payment, after the user clicks the main CTA.
- Required inputs: current visa type, visa expiry window, study completion status, occupation/intended occupation, English test status, and whether key evidence exists.
- Accepted field values: English test status = `done`, `booked`, `not booked`; key evidence status = `have most`, `have some`, `mostly missing`; study completion status = `completed`, `finishing soon`, `not completed`.
- Decision/output: one of three results: in-scope and proceed to paid intake, borderline and request manual confirmation, or out-of-scope and route away before payment.
- Decision rule: in-scope requires the target wedge plus completed or near-completed study, no obvious exclusion from the defined prep offer, and enough known facts to produce a prep summary; borderline means missing or unclear facts but plausible fit; out-of-scope means clearly outside the wedge or clearly requires licensed advice first.
- Edge cases: unclear answers, inconsistent answers, or missing evidence move to borderline rather than silently passing.
- Definition of done: the user knows whether to proceed, wait for review, or seek licensed help before paying.

### 2. Standardized Agent-Ready Summary Artifact
- Trigger: generated after successful paid intake and founder review.
- Owner: a designated case reviewer prepares and approves the artifact.
- Required inputs: structured intake fields, evidence status, provisional points assumptions, missing-document list, and next-step actions.
- Output format: fixed template, not freeform advice. Sections: user facts, assumptions, missing items, major uncertainties, next 3 actions, and disclaimer boundary.
- Edge cases: if evidence is missing or conflicting, the summary must mark the claim as unverified instead of inferring certainty.
- Delivery path: delivered by email or secure link as the single source-of-truth artifact for v1.
- Definition of done: the user receives one consistent artifact that is useful to both the user and any licensed reviewer.

### 3. Lightweight Status Visibility
- Trigger: starts immediately after payment.
- States: `received`, `reviewing`, `need more info`, `report ready`.
- Operating model: manual or semi-manual updates are acceptable in v1; no full dashboard required. A single case record is the source of truth, and the case reviewer updates state transitions with timestamps.
- Visibility path: show status on a confirmation page and mirror it in email updates.
- Edge cases: abandoned purchases stay unstarted, failed payments never create active review, duplicate submissions merge into one case record, and out-of-scope discoveries after payment route to the explicit service-rule policy below.
- Definition of done: the user is never left wondering whether the case disappeared into a manual inbox.

## Service Rules
- Turnaround target: 2 business days for in-scope cases after all required inputs are complete; the SLA pauses while waiting on missing user information.
- Refund rule: out-of-scope before review starts means full refund; out-of-scope discovered during review means credit only if a usable partial prep artifact was already produced, otherwise partial refund.
- Compliance boundary: the service may organize facts and prep questions, but may not claim legal eligibility or replace licensed migration advice.
- Handoff rule: licensed referral is optional in v1, not part of the core promise.
- Failed payment rule: failed payment returns the user to retry; no case review starts.
- No-response rule: if the user does not respond after `need more info`, the case stays paused and the SLA remains paused.

## Success Metrics
- Triage pass rate versus out-of-scope rate
- Refund rate after payment
- Average turnaround time for completed reports
- Percentage of paid users who receive a completed agent-ready summary
- User-reported confidence lift after receiving the summary

## Edge Cases
- Borderline fit: hold for manual confirmation before payment.
- Missing evidence: allow progress only if the summary clearly labels assumptions and gaps.
- User becomes out-of-scope after payment: route through refund policy and licensed escalation.
- Abandoned purchase: do not start review SLA until required inputs are complete.

## Design Addendum

### Product Type
- This is a `HYBRID` experience: trust-first entry screen that turns into a guided intake + status flow.
- The first viewport should obey landing-page composition rules.
- Triage, intake, status, and artifact sections should obey calmer app-layout rules.

### Information Hierarchy
- First screen hierarchy:
  1. Trust promise: what this service helps with and what it does not promise.
  2. Target-user cue: temporary graduate visa holders from Nepal already in Australia.
  3. Primary action: `Start my readiness check`.
  4. One sentence on what happens next and what the user will receive.
- Section map:
  - Hero: qualify the visitor, establish trust.
  - Triage intro: explain what will be asked and how long it takes.
  - Intake: collect only the facts needed for a prep summary.
  - Status tracker: show `received` → `reviewing` → `need more info` → `report ready`.
  - Artifact preview: show what the standardized summary looks like.
  - FAQ: answer trust, scope, and compliance concerns only.

### Emotional Tone
- First-screen tone: clarity-first reassurance, not hype.
- Status states should feel transparent and organized, not anxious.
- Artifact delivery should feel like organized next-step confidence.

### Layout Model
- Do not default to a card-stack layout for app-like portions.
- Desktop: split-pane or strong two-zone layout with clear progression.
- Mobile: stepper-like progression with one main action per step and explicit progress cues.
- Cards only where the card itself is the interaction.

### Interaction States
| Feature | Loading | Empty | Error | Success | Partial |
|--------|---------|-------|-------|---------|---------|
| Triage | Short loading state before showing the result | Not applicable | Contradictory or incomplete answers shown inline with recovery guidance | In-scope / borderline / out-of-scope result screen | Step progress visible while answering |
| Status tracker | Status polling or refresh indicator | Not applicable | Status retrieval failure shows last known state with stale indicator | Status update shown clearly with timestamp | In-progress states show progress context |
| Intake | Save/submit loading state with visible progress | Empty initial form with supportive microcopy | Explicit submit-failure state answers whether the case was saved | Confirmation that the case was received | Incomplete form preserves progress and error context |
| Artifact | Loading state while summary is being prepared | No-match state still presents the artifact as a useful self-summary | Artifact failure explains delay or next step | In-page summary as the payoff state | Missing evidence appears as clearly labeled gaps |

### AI Slop Guardrails

Do not use any of the following patterns in this service:
1. Purple/violet/indigo gradient backgrounds or blue-to-purple color schemes.
2. The 3-column feature grid: icon-in-colored-circle + bold title + 2-line description, repeated symmetrically.
3. Icons in colored circles as section decoration.
4. Centered everything (`text-align: center` on all headings, descriptions, cards).
5. Uniform bubbly border-radius on every element.
6. Decorative blobs, floating circles, wavy SVG dividers.
7. Emoji as design elements.
8. Colored left-border on cards.
9. Generic hero copy ("Welcome to...", "Unlock the power of...", "Your all-in-one solution for...").
10. Cookie-cutter section rhythm.

Service-specific watch-outs:
- Status tracker must not look like a generic order-status card — use mono language and factual tone.
- Triage result screens must use trust language, not gamification or motivational copy.
- Artifact summary should read like a structured document, not a dashboard widget.

### Design System Adaptation

- Preserve the current family from `DESIGN.md`: Fraunces, Instrument Sans, IBM Plex Mono, and the navy/eucalyptus/coral token palette.
- Adapt for this service:
  - more procedural hierarchy in intake and status sections
  - stronger use of mono/process language for status states and artifact sections
  - trust-first explanation above conversion pressure
  - fewer decorative landing-page gestures — this is a service, not a marketing page

### Responsive And Accessibility
- Mobile stepper: one main action per step, explicit progress cues.
- Clear landmarks and heading structure for screen readers.
- After each major transition, move focus intentionally to the new heading or result state.
- Minimum touch targets: 44px.
- Validation errors must be associated clearly with the affected field and summarized accessibly.

### Motion
- Use only three service-specific motions:
  - subtle hero proof stagger on entry
  - step transition between intake states
  - artifact reveal motion when the summary becomes available
- Avoid decorative or ambient motion beyond those three.

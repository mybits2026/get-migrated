# Team Briefing: PR Guidance V1

## What We Are Building

We are building a service-first product for Nepali students and recent graduates in Australia who are early in their PR journey and need clarity and prep guidance before final application-critical steps.

This is not a migration-agent replacement product.

The product helps users:
- understand what applies to them
- know what to do next
- organize prep work
- know when licensed expert review is needed

## Why This Exists

Right now users face two bad choices:

1. DIY through scattered advice from Facebook groups, YouTube, friends, and random sources
2. Pay a full-service migration agent thousands of dollars earlier than they want to

The gap is clear: users want a cheaper, clearer, safer prep path without pretending software can replace legal sign-off.

## Core Product Thesis

We can win by owning the clarity, preparation, and organization layer, then handing off to a licensed expert only when the risky step arrives.

We are unbundling a full-service migration process, not replacing it.

## Why The Scope Is Narrow

We deliberately narrowed V1 because trust is the bottleneck.

V1 is only for:
- Nepali students or recent graduates in Australia
- early-stage PR clarity and prep
- users who are not yet at final submission-heavy steps

We are not trying to serve every immigration case.
We are not trying to support every PR workflow.
We are not trying to automate legal advice.

## Main Decisions And Why

### Service-first, not software-first
Trust matters more than UI sophistication right now. We need to learn from real users before hardening the workflow into product.

### No-login V1
Accounts add complexity before the core workflow is proven. We keep operations simple first.

### No document upload in V1
This reduces privacy risk, compliance burden, and false expectations.

### One single source of truth tracker
We cannot let case state live across WhatsApp, memory, and random docs. Every case must be tracked in one place.

### Guided action plan, not a generic report
The user wants orientation and next steps, not a long information dump.

### Licensed expert review at the risky step
This keeps the legal/trust boundary clean and avoids pretending we offer final migration sign-off.

## What The User Will Experience

1. They find a landing page that clearly explains the offer
2. They complete a calm 3-step intake
3. We review whether they fit the V1 cohort
4. We create a guided action plan for supported users
5. They receive clear next steps, missing items, and guidance status
6. If needed, they are handed off to a licensed expert later

## What Makes This Trustworthy

The product must communicate these 4 things very clearly:
- built for Nepali students in Australia
- personalized action plan, not generic immigration content
- cheaper than full-service agents
- licensed expert review when needed

## Messaging Boundary

We help with:
- clarity
- preparation
- organization
- next steps
- expert review when needed

We do not claim:
- guaranteed PR outcomes
- final legal migration sign-off
- full migration-agent replacement

## How We Will Operate Internally

Each case will have:
- support eligibility decision
- internal triage
- roadmap version and expiry
- follow-up status
- expert handoff trigger if needed

Internal triage remains 3-tier:
- Green
- Yellow
- Red

User-facing wording is softer:
- Clear to continue with guided prep
- Needs careful review later
- Needs licensed review before continuing

## Validation Plan

Before real users:
- run 10 synthetic cases
- test support, triage, staleness, and handoff rules

During beta:
- manually serve the first 10 real users
- charge from the start
- cap active cases and use a waitlist if needed

## What Success Looks Like

We want to learn:
- do users trust the offer?
- do they pay for clarity/prep?
- do the same case patterns repeat enough to productize?
- do users clearly understand the legal boundary?

## What Would Make Us Pause

We stop and revise if:
- too few real users pay
- triage is inconsistent
- users think we are offering legal sign-off
- unsupported/complex cases dominate
- expert handoff is unreliable

## Immediate Next Step

We are no longer in broad strategy mode.

The next phase is execution:
- set up the tracker
- finalize intake and landing page
- run synthetic cases
- recruit first real beta users
- manually deliver action plans
